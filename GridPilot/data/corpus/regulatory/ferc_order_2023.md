## Section 1. Transition to First-Ready-First-Served Cluster Studies
Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC) Order No. 2023 institutes a shift from the historic first-come-first-served queue processing model to a first-ready-first-served cluster study methodology. Under this model, transmission providers must evaluate interconnection requests in large group clusters rather than individually, ensuring that system impacts are assessed globally across all projects seeking interconnection in a specific region during a defined window.

Interconnection customers are required to meet strict readiness milestones to enter and remain in the cluster study queue. These milestones include demonstrating control of the site, providing financial security deposits, and submitting proof of technological readiness.

## Section 2. Proportional Cost Allocation of Network Upgrades
The network upgrade costs identified during the cluster study process shall be allocated among all members of the study cluster on a proportional basis. Proportional allocation is determined by applying a system impact factor, which measures each project's contribution to thermal overloads, voltage stability issues, or short-circuit current increases on the transmission network.

Upgrade costs that benefit the entire cluster, such as common substation additions, will be split equally or based on capacity (MW) ratios. However, specific radial line upgrades that directly serve only a subset of projects will be allocated strictly to those projects using a direct-assignment model.

## Section 3. Financial Penalties for Interconnection Queue Withdrawal
To prevent speculative projects from blocking the interconnection queue, FERC Order 2023 establishes substantial financial penalties for projects that withdraw from the study process after a certain milestone. Withdrawal penalties are calculated based on a percentage of the customer's study deposit and increase as the project advances through the study phases.

Penalties collected from withdrawing projects are distributed first to offset the costs of re-studying the remaining members of the cluster, and second to reduce the net upgrade costs assigned to active projects in the same cluster.
