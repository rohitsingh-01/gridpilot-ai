## Section 1. Utility Interconnection Request Procedures
Any electrical generation facility seeking connection to the state distribution system must submit a formal Interconnection Request (IR) to the local electric utility. The IR must include the complete electrical specifications of the facility, including transformer ratings, inverter models, and reactive power capabilities.

The utility will perform an initial screening study within 30 days of receiving the IR to determine if the distribution system has sufficient thermal and voltage capacity to host the proposed generation capacity.

## Section 2. Interconnection Study Fees and Deposits
Customers must pay all actual costs associated with utility interconnection studies. A non-refundable application fee is required at submittal, followed by study deposits before the system impact study and facilities study phases begin.

| Phase | Deposit Required | Estimated Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| Application Screening | $5,000 | 30 Days |
| System Impact Study | $25,000 | 90 Days |
| Facilities Study | $50,000 | 120 Days |

Failure to submit the required study deposit within 15 business days of utility notification will result in automatic withdrawal of the Interconnection Request.

## Section 3. Distribution System Host Capacity Limits
The host capacity limit is the maximum amount of generation capacity that can be connected to a specific distribution circuit without violating safety, reliability, or power quality standards. If the host capacity limit is exceeded, the customer must fund necessary distribution system upgrades, such as reconductoring lines or upgrading substation transformers, before interconnection is approved.
