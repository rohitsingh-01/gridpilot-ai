## Section 1. Protected Wetland Resource Areas
The state Wetlands Protection Act regulates all activities within or adjacent to wetlands resource areas. Protected areas include freshwater wetlands, coastal wetlands, wet meadows, bogs, swamps, and areas subject to flooding.

Any activity that will remove, fill, dredge, or alter a protected wetland resource area requires a formal Notice of Intent (NOI) and must receive an Order of Conditions (OOC) from the local conservation commission before construction begins.

## Section 2. Wetland Buffer Zone Requirements
A 100-foot buffer zone extends horizontally from the delineated boundary of any protected wetland resource area. Within the buffer zone, certain activities are subject to review to prevent degradation of wetland functions (such as water filtration, flood control, and wildlife habitat).

- **0 to 50 Feet (No-Disturb Zone):** No permanent structures, grading, or vegetation clearing is permitted, except for path maintenance or invasive species control.
- **50 to 100 Feet (Review Zone):** Light construction, utility conduits, and access roads may be approved if the applicant demonstrates that no viable alternative exists and that appropriate sedimentation control measures will be active.

## Section 3. Wetland Mitigation and Replication Ratios
If impacts to wetland resource areas are unavoidable, the applicant must construct a replacement wetland area (wetland replication) to offset the loss. Wetland replication must occur at a minimum ratio of 1.5:1 (meaning 1.5 square feet of replication for every 1 square foot of impacted wetland).

Replication areas must be located adjacent to existing wetland systems, use appropriate wetland soils, and be planted with native wetland plant species. The replication area must be monitored for at least five years to ensure successful establishment.
