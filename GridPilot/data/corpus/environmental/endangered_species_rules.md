## Section 1. Determination of Critical Habitats
The Endangered Species Act (ESA) requires federal agencies to ensure that any action they authorize, fund, or carry out is not likely to jeopardize the continued existence of listed species or result in the destruction or adverse modification of designated critical habitat.

Critical habitats are specific geographic areas that contain physical or biological features essential to the conservation of a threatened or endangered species and that may require special management considerations or protection.

## Section 2. Seasonal Construction Restrictions and Closures
To minimize disturbance during sensitive life stages of listed species, construction activities within or adjacent to critical habitats are subject to seasonal restrictions:

- **Avian Nesting Season (March 15 to August 15):** No vegetation clearing, grading, or heavy machinery operations are permitted within 500 feet of active nests of protected migratory bird species.
- **Bat Roosting Season (June 1 to July 31):** No tree removal or clearing is permitted in forest areas identified as potential roosting habitat for endangered bat species.

## Section 3. Conservation Offsets and Mitigation Banks
If project construction results in permanent alteration of critical habitat, the applicant must purchase conservation credits from an approved mitigation bank or establish a permanent conservation easement on equivalent land.

The standard mitigation offset ratio is 2:1 (meaning two acres of habitat must be protected for every one acre impacted). Offsets must support the same species and ecological community affected by the project.
